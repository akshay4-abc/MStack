The Quality of Mercy by Faye Kellerman: A direct and inventive retelling of Shakespeare's classic. The story follows a headstrong Jewish woman in Elizabethan London who, disguised as a man, plunges into the gritty underground with the playwright Will Shakespeare himself.
The Count of Monte Cristo by Alexandre Dumas: A masterful tale of wealth, extreme debts, and elaborate revenge. While it doesn't take place in Venice, it explores the deep philosophical lines between justice, mercy, and the price of vengeance in society.
The Merchant Of Venice by William Shakespeare: The Merchant of Venice follows Antonio, a merchant who borrows money from a Jewish moneylender, Shylock, to help his friend Bassanio woo the wealthy heiress Portia. When Antonio defaults on the loan, Shylock demands a gruesome penalty: a pound of Antonio's flesh.
